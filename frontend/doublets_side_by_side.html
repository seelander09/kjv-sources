Creation Stories
Two different creation accounts in Genesis
P
Genesis 1:1-2:3 (Priestly)
1In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth. 2And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters. 3And God said, Let there be light: and there was light. 4And God saw the light, that it was good: and God divided the light from the darkness. 5And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And the evening and the morning were the first day...

[Systematic creation over seven days, emphasizing divine command and order. God creates through speaking. Culminates in Sabbath rest.]
J
Genesis 2:4b-25 (Jahwist)
4bIn the day that the LORD God made the earth and the heavens, 5And every plant of the field before it was in the earth, and every herb of the field before it grew: for the LORD God had not caused it to rain upon the earth, and there was not a man to till the ground. 7And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul...

[Anthropomorphic account focusing on human creation. God forms with hands, plants a garden, creates woman from man's rib. More narrative and personal.]

Key Theological Differences:

  • Divine Name: Elohim (God) vs. Yahweh Elohim (LORD God)
  • Creation Order: Cosmos first vs. humans first
  • Divine Method: Word/command vs. hands-on formation
  • Human Creation: Image of God vs. dust of the ground
  • Structure: Seven-day framework vs. single day narrative

P
Genesis 6:9-22; 7:8-9,11,13-16a,21,24 (Priestly)
6:9These are the generations of Noah: Noah was a just man and perfect in his generations, and Noah walked with God. 11The earth also was corrupt before God, and the earth was filled with violence. 13And God said unto Noah, The end of all flesh is come before me; for the earth is filled with violence through them; and, behold, I will destroy them with the earth...

7:11In the six hundredth year of Noah's life, in the second month, the seventeenth day of the month, the same day were all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened...

[Precise chronology, ark specifications, covenant emphasis, systematic dates]

Key Theological Differences:

  • Animal Numbers: 7 pairs clean, 2 unclean (J) vs. 2 of each kind (P)
  • Duration: 40 days of rain (J) vs. precise 1-year chronology (P)
  • Divine Character: Emotional, regretful God (J) vs. transcendent, systematic God (P)
  • Focus: Moral corruption (J) vs. systematic destruction and renewal (P)
Wife-Sister Motif
Three accounts of patriarch presenting wife as sister
J
Genesis 12:10-20 (Abraham/Sarah in Egypt)
10And there was a famine in the land: and Abram went down into Egypt to sojourn there; for the famine was grievous in the land. 11And it came to pass, when he was come near to enter into Egypt, that he said unto Sarai his wife, Behold now, I know that thou art a fair woman to look upon: 12Therefore it shall come to pass, when the Egyptians shall see thee, that they shall say, This is his wife: and they will kill me, but they will save thee alive. 13Say, I pray thee, thou art my sister: that it may be well with me for thy sake; and my soul shall live because of thee...

[Pharaoh takes Sarah, plagues follow, Abraham expelled with gifts]
E
Genesis 20:1-18 (Abraham/Sarah with Abimelech)

Ten Commandments
Multiple versions of the Decalogue
R
Exodus 20:1-17 (Ethical Decalogue)
1And God spake all these words, saying, 2I am the LORD thy God, which have brought thee out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage. 3Thou shalt have no other gods before me. 4Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image... 8Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy... 13Thou shalt not kill. 14Thou shalt not commit adultery. 15Thou shalt not steal. 16Thou shalt not bear false witness...

D
Deuteronomy 5:6-18 (Deuteronomic Version)
6I am the LORD thy God, which brought thee out of the land of Egypt, from the house of bondage. 7Thou shalt have none other gods before me. 8Thou shalt not make thee any graven image... 12Keep the sabbath day to sanctify it, as the LORD thy God hath commanded thee... 17Thou shalt not kill. 18Neither shalt thou commit adultery...

[Similar content but with Deuteronomic expansions and explanations, sabbath reasoning differs (creation vs. Egyptian bondage)]

Key Differences:

  • Sabbath Rationale: Creation rest (Exodus) vs. remembering slavery (Deuteronomy)
  • Style: Concise commands vs. expanded explanations
  • Context: Sinai revelation vs. Plains of Moab review
  • Emphasis: Universal principles vs. covenant renewal
